# paytm-onboarding-case-study
# Improving Paytm Onboarding Experience

## Problem Statement

New users on Paytm often face friction during onboarding due to multiple steps like mobile verification, KYC requirements, and a feature-heavy interface. This leads to confusion and drop-offs before users complete their first transaction.

**Goal:**
Increase the number of users who complete their first transaction by simplifying onboarding and reducing friction.

---

## User Persona

**Segment:** First-time digital payment users (Tier 2/3 cities)

* Age: 18–40
* Needs: Simple, fast, and trustworthy experience
* Pain Points:

  * Too many options on first screen
  * Confusion about what to do next
  * Hesitation to link bank account or complete KYC

---

## Current User Flow

1. User installs the app
2. Enters mobile number → OTP verification
3. Lands on a feature-heavy home screen
4. Sees prompts for bank linking / KYC
5. Gets confused or exits without completing a transaction

---

## Key Problems Identified

**1. Feature Overload**
Too many options (recharge, UPI, insurance, loans) shown immediately → decision fatigue

**2. No Clear Guidance**
Users are not guided toward completing their first transaction

**3. KYC Confusion**
Users don’t understand why KYC is needed or when to do it

**4. Trust Gap**
First-time users hesitate to link bank accounts without reassurance

---

## Proposed Solutions

### 1. Guided Onboarding Flow

Introduce a step-by-step onboarding experience:

* Step 1: Mobile verification
* Step 2: Prompt to complete first small transaction (e.g., ₹1)
* Step 3: Bank linking with clear instructions
* Step 4: KYC prompt only when required

---

### 2. First Transaction Incentive

Offer a small cashback reward (₹10–₹50) for completing the first payment

→ Encourages action and builds confidence

---

### 3. Simplified Home Screen (for new users)

Show only 3 key actions initially:

* Send Money
* Scan & Pay
* Mobile Recharge

Gradually introduce other features later

---

### 4. Contextual KYC Messaging

Instead of generic prompts:

* “Complete KYC to unlock higher transaction limits”
* Short explanation of benefits

---

### 5. Trust Building Elements

* Show “Trusted by millions of users”
* Highlight security and safety messaging
* Use simple and reassuring language

---

## Success Metrics

**Primary Metrics:**

* Increase in first transaction completion rate (Activation Rate)
* Reduction in onboarding drop-offs

**Secondary Metrics:**

* Time to first transaction
* KYC completion rate
* Day 1 retention

---

## Experiment Plan

* **Control Group:** Current onboarding flow
* **Test Group:** Guided onboarding flow

Measure:

* % of users completing first transaction
* % of users linking bank account
* Retention after 24–48 hours

---

## Expected Impact

* Higher activation rate (more users completing first payment)
* Reduced confusion for new users
* Improved trust and retention

---

## Key Takeaways

* Simplicity improves user adoption
* First successful experience is critical
* Guided flows are more effective than feature-heavy interfaces
* Trust and clarity are essential in fintech products
